FAW Life Cycle

The Fall armyworm, Spodoptera frugiperda, is a major invasive pest in Africa.
It has a voracious appetite and feeds on more than 80 plant species, including maize, rice, sorghum and sugarcane. Another feature which makes it an incredibly successful invasive species is its ability to spread and reproduce quickly.
Food and Agriculture Organisation have developed a poster to show the life cycle of the Fall armyworm, which includes egg, 6 growth stages of caterpillar development (instars), pupa and adult moth.
